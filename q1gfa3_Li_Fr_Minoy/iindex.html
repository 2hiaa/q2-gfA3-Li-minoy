Introduction


Women rights have been improving over the years, an effect of the modernization of the world. Women empowerment has severely changed the way society works right now in comparison to the past. Today, women have more liberties from unrealistic and stereotypical standards; women are seen as equals to men, and are not denied opportunities due to their sex. However, in spite of the progress of this movement, some women are hindered until today. Our project aims to showcase women rights in different countries in Oceania and Asia. We will present six variables and interpret the data to come to a conclusion.

Our data was taken from multiple sources. From the 48 countries in Asia, only 46 are present in the data set. From the 14 countries in Oceania, only 6 are present in the data set. The variables that are present are the country classification (developed, developing, least developing), women civil liberties, women property rights, participation of women in politics, women's access to the justice system, and women's right to vote.
